Head of Marketing Signpost Six 2023—2026

Built the whole marketing engine — and learned every part of it.

Signpost Six is a niche B2B cybersecurity consultancy in a category most buyers don't know exists: insider risk. There was no commercial infrastructure, no content strategy, no demand engine when I joined — which meant I got to build (and learn) every piece of it.

Over three years I built the full commercial system: HubSpot (lifecycle stages, dashboards, segmentation, workflows), a content and thought-leadership program (Insider Risk Trend Report, Self-Assessment Tool), and full-funnel campaigns that fed the pipeline. A lot of Googling, a lot of iterating, a lot of "let me figure out why this report won't pull the right data." I'm proud of the result — and probably more proud of how much I learned getting there.

How I think

Four things I've come to believe.

Opinions and frameworks from six years of figuring marketing out across B2B and B2C. Subject to change — that's kind of the point.

01

HubSpot works best when you treat it as a system, not a CRM.

Most teams use a fraction of what HubSpot can do and then wonder why it feels expensive. I've learned the hard way that it's really the connective tissue between marketing, sales, and product — lifecycle stages, pipeline logic, attribution. When those pieces talk to each other, the whole revenue engine gets easier.

02

Partnerships work better as a hybrid play.

At Ekster I ended up building affiliate strategy as PR × influencer × performance — one network, three monetization angles. It's how we landed in buying guides, review content, and high-intent commerce environments at the same time. Siloing partnerships into separate categories seems to leave a lot of value on the table.

03

Thought leadership is only worth it when it earns a conversation.

The Insider Risk Trend Report at Signpost Six wasn't a whitepaper — it ended up being a reason for a CISO to book a call. My rule of thumb now: every asset should have a commercial job it's trying to do. If it's not creating pipeline, research, or press, it's probably a vanity exercise.

04

If I can't explain how it works, I haven't learned it yet.

I'd rather spend an extra week figuring out why a HubSpot workflow misfires, or how Cloudflare DNS delegation actually routes a subdomain, than hand it off as a mystery. Curiosity is the edge. Most of what I'm good at, I'm good at because I got nerdy about it first.
